# Comparison Mode

## Purpose
Force the framework to compare itself with neighboring explanations before claiming priority.

This file is one of the main upgrades in v3.0.

---

## Rule
Before finalizing a structural-Marxist answer, ask:
**Which framework best explains the binding mechanism here?**

---

## Comparison grid

### Economics
Best when:
- incentives
- price signals
- principal-agent problems
- strategic interaction
- competition
- equilibrium effects

Marxist frame adds value when:
- incentives are embedded in durable dependency or ownership structures
- value capture and reproduction matter
- apparent “market outcomes” are historically conditioned

### Psychology
Best when:
- cognition
- bias
- emotion
- motivation
- identity threat
- interpersonal attachment

Marxist frame adds value when:
- “psychology” is being produced by chronic structural conditions
- agency is shaped by institutions, labor conditions, or platform design

### Management / Organization Theory
Best when:
- coordination
- governance
- incentive alignment
- routines
- information flow
- accountability design

Marxist frame adds value when:
- coordination problems reproduce hierarchy or extraction
- governance cannot be understood apart from power and dependence

### Law / Policy
Best when:
- formal rights and obligations
- enforcement
- jurisdiction
- administrative process
- regulatory authority

Marxist frame adds value when:
- the legal form hides economic power or institutional asymmetry
- rules stabilize an unequal structure while appearing neutral

### Engineering / Systems
Best when:
- technical bottlenecks
- reliability
- throughput
- architecture constraints
- measurement design

Marxist frame adds value when:
- technical choices encode incentives or governance
- the system’s architecture distributes costs and control unevenly

---

## Decision outputs

### Dominant
Another framework clearly does most of the work.
Use Marxist analysis only as context.

### Hybrid
Two frameworks jointly explain the case.
This is common and often best.

### Structural-dominant
The Marxist-derived frame identifies the reproduction mechanism better than rivals.
Use it centrally, but still acknowledge what the others explain.

---

## Good sentence patterns
- “The immediate mechanism is psychological, but the persistence of the problem is structural.”
- “Economics explains the incentives; this framework explains why those incentives are organized this way and why they keep reproducing.”
- “Engineering explains the bottleneck, but not why the organization keeps selecting this architecture despite the known costs.”
