# Ideology Critique

## Core question
Which framing makes the arrangement look normal, deserved, neutral, or unavoidable?

---

## Procedure

### 1. Name the dominant framing
Examples:
- “the market has spoken”
- “this is just merit”
- “the metric is objective”
- “users simply prefer it”

### 2. Separate truth from function
A framing may be partially true and still ideological.

### 3. Ask what it hides
- labor
- dependency
- externalized costs
- institutional design
- coercion disguised as choice

### 4. Ask what coordination problem it solves
Why is this framing useful to institutions or elites?
Why is it easy for ordinary actors to repeat?

### 5. Offer a better mechanism description
Replace accusation with clearer explanation.

---

## Output template
- **Dominant framing**
- **What it captures**
- **What it conceals**
- **Whose interests it stabilizes**
- **Better description**

---

## Anti-conspiracy rule
Ideology critique should explain how misleading beliefs can be socially functional **without** assuming secret centralized manipulation.
