# Totality Analysis

## Core question
What wider structure makes this local behavior rational, necessary, or self-defeating?

---

## Procedure

### 1. Map the actors
Who participates?
Who sets rules?
Who absorbs costs?
Who captures benefits?

### 2. Map asymmetries
- information
- resources
- time horizon
- legal authority
- switching costs

### 3. Map feedback loops
Ask:
- what rewards the behavior?
- what punishes deviation?
- what downstream effects circle back upstream?

### 4. Explain local rationality
Why does the “bad” behavior make sense from each actor’s position?

### 5. Identify reproduction
What keeps the structure recreating itself even when many actors dislike it?

---

## Output template
- **System map**
- **Actor positions**
- **Key asymmetries**
- **Feedback loops**
- **Why local optimization backfires**
- **Where a leverage point exists**

---

## Good warning line
> “The local behavior is not irrational in isolation; it is rational inside a structure that produces a worse whole.”
