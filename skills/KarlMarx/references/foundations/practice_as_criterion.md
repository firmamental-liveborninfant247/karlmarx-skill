# Practice as Criterion

## Core idea
An explanation is not fully adequate because it sounds deep, morally satisfying, or conceptually elegant.
It must survive contact with practice.

This category connects analysis to:
- intervention,
- observation,
- revision,
- and consequences.

---

## Why this matters
Without this category, the package risks turning into:
- theory display,
- rhetorical critique,
- or insulated interpretation.

This category asks:
What would we do differently if this analysis were correct?
What observable result would support or weaken it?

---

## Questions to ask
- What intervention follows from this diagnosis?
- What would count as confirmation?
- What would count as disconfirmation?
- Who would need to act?
- What resistance or friction would appear in practice?

---

## Analytical use
Weak:
> “The issue is structural.”

Stronger:
> “If the issue is really structural, then changing only individual incentives should underperform compared with changing the decision-rights and feedback system. That is the next thing to test.”

---

## Practical rule
Every strong answer should be able to name:
1. one intervention,
2. one observable indicator,
3. one revision condition.

---

## Misuse to avoid
### 1. Action theater
Do not propose interventions that are only slogans.

### 2. Test-free abstraction
Do not stop at diagnosis.

### 3. Immediate-results bias
Some structural claims need longer observation; not all failures of instant change disprove the diagnosis.

---

## Best fit
Use when:
- the user wants strategy or action
- multiple explanations are competing
- the answer risks becoming too theoretical

---

## Plain-language translation
- practice as criterion -> test the explanation in action and feedback
