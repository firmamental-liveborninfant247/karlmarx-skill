# Mediation and Levels

## Core idea
Real systems rarely move directly from macro structure to local symptom in one jump.
They operate through **mediations**: intermediate mechanisms, institutional layers, interfaces, routines, metrics, norms, and transmission chains.

This category forces the answer to show the path between levels.

---

## What mistake it corrects
It corrects leap-analysis.
Typical weak move:
- “Capitalism caused this feature bug.”
- “Management culture explains every team problem.”

The stronger question is:
**Through what intermediate levels and mechanisms does the wider structure become this concrete outcome?**

---

## Runtime use
Use this category when:
- the answer jumps from a big structure to a small event
- the case spans user, team, platform, market, and policy levels
- the user needs a causal chain, not only a macro label
- leverage depends on locating the right intermediate layer

---

## Control questions
1. What levels are operating here: individual, team, organization, platform, market, policy?
2. What mediating mechanisms connect them?
3. Where does the translation from one level to another occur?
4. Which level is currently the bottleneck?
5. Which level offers the highest-leverage intervention?

---

## Example
A platform's growth imperative does not directly produce user burnout.
The mediation may run through product goals, notification policy, creator incentives, ranking design, and daily behavioral loops.

---

## Good output move
> “The macro pressure matters, but only through the mediating chain of KPIs, product decisions, and user-habit design. That mediating layer is where explanation becomes actionable.”

---

## Warning
Do not make mediation an excuse for endless complexity.
Its point is not to multiply layers, but to identify the decisive chain linking structure to effect.
