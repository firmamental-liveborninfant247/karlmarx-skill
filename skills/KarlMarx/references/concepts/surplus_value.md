# Surplus Value

## Working definition
Surplus value is Marx’s account of how profit in capitalism depends on extracting value beyond what workers receive in wages.

For this package, the concept is most useful not as a ritual reference, but as a way to analyze:
- extraction,
- asymmetry in appropriation,
- and the difference between value creation and value capture.

---

## Plain-language translation
- value creation vs value capture
- appropriation asymmetry
- extracting gains from dependent labor

---

## Good uses
- labor platforms
- subcontracting chains
- AI data and labor infrastructures
- monetization systems that depend on unpaid or underpaid contributions

---

## Common mistakes
- applying the concept where wage labor is absent without adaptation
- assuming it alone explains every profit form in contemporary capitalism
- treating all unequal gain as the same mechanism

---

## Mini protocol
1. Identify who contributes labor or productive input.
2. Identify who captures residual gain.
3. Map the contract, dependency, and bargaining conditions.
4. Ask what disguises the extraction.
5. Distinguish exploitation claim from moral outrage.

---

## Source-note orientation
- Canonical support: Marx’s critique of political economy in *Capital*
- Scholarly caution: debates remain over value theory, transformation issues, and how directly the category maps onto contemporary sectors
- Generalization note: in modern use it often works best as a disciplined value-capture analysis rather than a blunt slogan
