# Foundational Moves Selector

## Purpose
Select the foundational philosophical moves that should discipline the answer.

This file prevents two opposite failures:
- using no foundational layer at all
- dumping all foundational categories into every answer

---

## Step 1: Identify the likely error mode

| Error mode in the draft answer | Foundational move to add |
|---|---|
| treats issue as isolated | relation and totality |
| treats present as fixed | movement and rest |
| describes sequence but not transformation | development and change |
| speaks too generally | universality and particularity |
| repeats symptoms without mechanism | essence and appearance |
| assigns one simple cause | cause and effect |
| sounds deterministic | necessity and contingency |
| misses thresholds | quantity and quality |
| confuses reform with transformation | negation and transformation |
| sounds insightful but untestable | practice as criterion |
| trusts labels, shells, or official design too quickly | content and form |
| confuses wish, scenario, and real possibility | possibility and actuality |
| swings between fatalism and voluntarism | freedom and necessity |
| stays generic instead of returning to the case | abstraction and concretion |
| jumps from macro structure to local symptom | mediation and levels |

---

## Step 2: Pick two to four moves
For most answers, choose only two to four foundational categories.

Recommended default set for general structural analysis:
1. relation and totality
2. movement and rest
3. essence and appearance
4. practice as criterion

Common strong additions:
- content and form
- abstraction and concretion
- mediation and levels
- possibility and actuality

---

## Step 3: Use them as control questions

### Relation and totality
What wider relation gives this local event its logic?

### Movement and rest
What is changing beneath the apparent stability?

### Development and change
Is this merely sequence, or a structured transformation?

### Universality and particularity
Which broader mechanism applies, and how is this case different?

### Essence and appearance
What visible symptom is being generated by what deeper mechanism?

### Cause and effect
What trigger-condition-feedback process is operating?

### Necessity and contingency
What was constrained, and what remained open?

### Quantity and quality
Is gradual accumulation nearing a threshold?

### Negation and transformation
Is the old form being adjusted, negated, or reorganized?

### Practice as criterion
What intervention would test this diagnosis?

### Content and form
What visible shell is present, and what operative relation actually fills it?

### Possibility and actuality
What is imaginable, what is really open, and what conditions would convert one into the other?

### Freedom and necessity
Which constraints are binding, and what room for agency remains within them?

### Abstraction and concretion
What simplifying model am I using, and which concrete determinations must be restored?

### Mediation and levels
Through which intermediate mechanisms does the wider structure become this concrete outcome?

---

## Output rule
Do not name all categories explicitly in the final answer.
Use them to sharpen the reasoning.
Only surface the philosophical language when:
- the user asks for it,
- teaching is the point,
- or the term materially improves precision.
