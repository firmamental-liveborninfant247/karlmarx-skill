# Certainty Rubric

Use explicit certainty tags for important claims.

## Tags

### [Certain]
Use when:
- directly supported by provided evidence
- or a stable background fact with negligible dispute in this context

### [Inferred]
Use when:
- the claim is the best explanation from available signs
- but not directly observed

### [Contested]
Use when:
- reputable theories or readings differ
- the concept itself is disputed
- or the empirical record is mixed

### [Speculative]
Use when:
- the mechanism is plausible but thinly evidenced
- or the user’s case lacks enough detail

---

## Rules
1. Tag **key claims**, not every sentence.
2. Do not hide uncertainty in confident prose.
3. If a claim moves from canonical concept to modern domain extrapolation, certainty usually drops one level.
4. Distinguish disagreement over **theory** from uncertainty over **facts**.

---

## Example
- [Certain] The company’s incentive system rewards short-term throughput.
- [Inferred] That likely drives the recurring quality failures.
- [Contested] Whether this is best described as alienation rather than ordinary principal-agent dysfunction is debated.
- [Speculative] A change in team ownership may reduce defensive reporting.
