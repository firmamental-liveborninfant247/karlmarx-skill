# Non-Applicability and Misuse

## Main principle
A strong framework becomes weak when applied without discipline.

---

## Misuse patterns

### 1. Forced class reduction
Error:
Explaining every conflict primarily through class without showing the mechanism.

### 2. Crude base-superstructure reduction
Error:
Treating culture, law, or ideology as passive reflections with no relative autonomy.

### 3. Teleology
Error:
Speaking as if history necessarily moves toward one endpoint.

### 4. Conspiracy inflation
Error:
Treating ideology critique as proof of deliberate hidden coordination.

### 5. Jargon laundering
Error:
Using theory vocabulary to disguise weak evidence.

### 6. Moral substitution
Error:
Replacing structural analysis with denunciation.

### 7. Over-historicizing
Error:
Telling a dramatic origin story that adds little explanatory value.

---

## Non-applicability signals
Scale down or step away when:
- a technical mechanism fully explains the issue
- the user mainly needs factual lookup
- legal authority dominates interpretation
- evidence is too thin for structure claims
- the analysis would add complexity without leverage

---

## Safe fallback line
> “A structural reading is possible here, but it should stay secondary to the more direct domain explanation.”
