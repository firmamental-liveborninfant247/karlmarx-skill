# Praxis

## Working definition
Praxis is the **unity of explanation and intervention**.

It is not mere action, and not mere contemplation.
It is analysis disciplined by the question: what action would test, revise, or transform the situation?

---

## Plain-language translation
- intervention-feedback loop
- theory tested in practice
- diagnosis linked to action
- learn-by-changing

---

## Good uses
- when the user wants leverage, not just interpretation
- when rival explanations need a practical test
- when structural critique risks becoming abstract

---

## Common mistakes
- reducing praxis to activism rhetoric
- assuming action alone validates theory
- ignoring whether the actor actually has capacity to intervene
- proposing maximal transformation where a small probe would teach more

---

## Mini protocol
1. State the diagnosis.
2. Identify the smallest meaningful intervention.
3. Define observable indicators.
4. Predict likely resistance.
5. Decide what result would falsify or refine the diagnosis.

---

## Source-note orientation
- Canonical support: Marx’s *Theses on Feuerbach*; broad praxis traditions in Marxism
- Scholarly caution: “praxis” has multiple lineages and should not be treated as one settled doctrine
- Generalization note: for modern analytical use, praxis is best operationalized as testable intervention rather than as slogan
