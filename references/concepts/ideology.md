# Ideology

## Working definition
Ideology is a way of representing social reality that helps make a given arrangement appear normal, deserved, neutral, or unavoidable.

This does **not** mean every mistaken belief is ideology, and it does **not** require conscious deception.

---

## Plain-language translation
- naturalized framing
- legitimating narrative
- selective common sense
- perception shaped by social position and institutions

---

## What it helps reveal
- why surface explanations persist
- how categories and metrics hide dependency relations
- why people defend arrangements that constrain them
- how “neutral” language can stabilize unequal structures

---

## Important caution
Do not collapse:
- ideology
- reification
- fetishism
- hegemony
- propaganda

These overlap but are not identical.

---

## Common mistakes
- assuming ideology means “lies told on purpose”
- treating every official explanation as false
- turning critique into conspiracy thinking
- ignoring the practical truth a partial ideology may still contain

---

## Mini protocol
1. Name the dominant framing.
2. Ask what it highlights.
3. Ask what it omits.
4. Ask whose coordination problem it solves.
5. Offer a better description of the mechanism.

---

## Source-note orientation
- Canonical support: Marx and Engels on ideology; Marx on commodity fetishism; Gramsci on hegemony; Althusser on ideological apparatuses
- Scholarly caution: the concept has both critical and descriptive uses, and later Marxist traditions do not all mean the same thing by it
- Generalization note: in applied work, ideology often maps best to framing, legitimation, and category construction
