# Foundational Philosophical Layer Overview

## Why this layer exists
The package already had strong application-level engines:
- contradiction analysis
- totality analysis
- historicization
- ideology critique
- praxis loop

What it lacked in earlier versions was a sufficiently explicit **foundational layer**.
That mattered because a general analytical engine should not merely *use* Marxist habits of thought.
It should be able to name, select, and discipline the basic philosophical moves that make those habits possible.

This layer provides that base.

---

## What this layer now contains
The foundational layer now has two sub-families.

### A. Core anti-static categories
- relation and totality
- movement and rest
- development and change
- universality and particularity
- essence and appearance
- cause and effect
- necessity and contingency
- quantity and quality
- negation and transformation
- practice as criterion

### B. High-leverage methodological weapons
- content and form
- possibility and actuality
- freedom and necessity
- abstraction and concretion
- mediation and levels

The second family matters because strong answers often fail not by missing a grand theory term, but by missing an efficient methodological correction.

---

## What mistake each family prevents
### Family A mainly prevents:
- atomism
- snapshot thinking
- surface empiricism
- deterministic overclaim
- gradualism blindness
- rhetoric without practice

### Family B mainly prevents:
- taking labels at face value
- confusing imagination with real possibility
- oscillating between fatalism and voluntarism
- generic abstraction that never returns to the case
- leap-analysis that skips intermediate mechanisms

---

## How to use this layer
Use the foundational layer in three ways.

### 1. As a pre-analysis screen
Ask:
- Which philosophical mistake is most likely here?
- Which foundational move would correct it fastest?

### 2. As a repair toolkit
If an answer sounds shallow, identify what is missing:
- no relations mapped -> relation and totality
- too static -> movement and rest
- too descriptive -> essence and appearance
- too label-trusting -> content and form
- too abstract -> abstraction and concretion
- too macro-jumpy -> mediation and levels
- too deterministic or too wishful -> possibility and actuality / freedom and necessity

### 3. As a prioritization aid
Not every case needs more concepts.
Sometimes one added foundational move does more than three added topical engines.

---

## Selection rule
Do not mechanically invoke all foundational categories.
Use only the ones that sharpen the analysis.

Strong answers usually rely on **two to four** foundational moves.
Complex strategic answers may use **four to six** if the structure genuinely requires it.

---

## Minimal runtime pattern
For complex social or organizational questions, a strong default pattern is:
1. relation and totality
2. movement and rest
3. essence and appearance
4. practice as criterion

When the case is especially strategic or implementation-heavy, commonly useful additions are:
- mediation and levels
- content and form
- abstraction and concretion

---

## Warning
This layer should make the package:
- more explicit,
- more transferable,
- more disciplined.

It should **not** make the package more scholastic, more jargon-heavy, or more textbook-performative.
