# Cause and Effect

## Core idea
Causes and effects are rarely simple one-way arrows in social systems.
They often involve:
- feedback loops,
- delayed effects,
- reciprocal influence,
- enabling conditions,
- and threshold effects.

This category deepens causal reasoning without abandoning causality.

---

## Why this matters
Weak analysis often says:
- X caused Y
- one actor caused the failure
- one policy caused the outcome

This category asks:
- Which causes are direct, indirect, background, enabling, or triggering?
- Which effects feed back into their own causes?
- What kind of causal process is actually operating?

---

## Questions to ask
- What is the immediate trigger?
- What background conditions made the trigger effective?
- Which effects feed back and reinforce the cause?
- Which relation is causal, and which is merely correlated?
- Is the key process linear, circular, layered, or delayed?

---

## Analytical use
Examples:
- incentive changes alter behavior, which then changes metrics, which then justifies further incentive changes
- understaffing lowers quality, poor quality raises churn, churn raises pressure, pressure deepens understaffing

---

## Causal discipline
Separate:
- trigger
- condition
- mechanism
- feedback
- outcome

That prevents over-simplification.

---

## Misuse to avoid
### 1. Correlation inflation
Do not rename association as cause.

### 2. Single-cause fantasy
Do not erase interacting causes.

### 3. System mysticism
Do not replace clear mechanism with vague “everything affects everything.”

---

## Best fit
Use when:
- the issue has loops or delays
- blame is being assigned too quickly
- one cause is being over-credited

---

## Plain-language translation
- cause and effect -> causal process / trigger-condition-mechanism loop
