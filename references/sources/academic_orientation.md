# Academic Orientation

## Goal
Keep the package academically sober without turning it into an encyclopedia.

---

## Minimal scholarly stance

1. There is **no single uncontested Marxist method**.
2. Core concepts such as historical materialism, ideology, alienation, and dialectics have important internal disputes.
3. The package therefore uses Marxist traditions as a **family of analytical tools**, not as a monolithic doctrine.
4. Modern application to product, AI, organization, education, and platform questions is an **interpretive extension**, not a claim that Marx directly theorized those cases.

---

## What counts as academically responsible here

- no invented textual claims
- no fake certainty about disputed interpretations
- no reduction of all later traditions to “what Marx really meant”
- no pretending that Gramsci, Althusser, Frankfurt School, and analytical Marxism are interchangeable
- no rigid stage theory presented as obvious fact
- no careless use of base/superstructure as a one-direction machine

---

## What this package prioritizes
Because this is a skill package rather than a monograph, it prioritizes:
- conceptual fidelity
- explicit caution around disputes
- high transferability to modern problems
- runtime usefulness
