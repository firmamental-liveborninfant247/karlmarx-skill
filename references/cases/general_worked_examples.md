# General Worked Examples

## 1. Product growth vs trust

### Surface
A platform grows quickly, but user trust deteriorates.

### Structural read
The product is organized around low-friction growth metrics while trust costs are delayed, diffuse, and borne by other actors.

### Principal contradiction
Scale velocity vs trust-preserving friction.

### Why it reproduces
Growth is immediately rewarded; trust damage is lagged and partially externalized.

### Leverage point
Change the metric stack so teams internalize downstream trust costs.

## 2. Education and “student underperformance”

### Surface
Students appear unmotivated.

### Structural read
Evaluation is organized around narrow measurable outputs, while students face fragmented time, unequal preparation, and weak ownership over learning.

### Principal contradiction
Standardized accountability vs meaningful learning.

### Leverage point
Shift one assessment sequence from high-stakes score production to iterative mastery tracking and measure whether engagement patterns change.

---

## 3. AI rollout inside an organization

### Surface
Employees resist a new AI system.

### Structural read
The system is presented as productivity support, but workers interpret it through existing power asymmetries: surveillance, deskilling, and accountability transfer.

### Principal contradiction
Efficiency gains for management vs autonomy/security for workers.

### Leverage point
Separate augmentation workflows from performance-evaluation workflows and test adoption differences.

---

## 4. Personal burnout with structural component

### Surface
A person feels like they are failing at self-discipline.

### Structural read
Their schedule, income volatility, and platform-mediated work rhythm systematically destroy recovery and planning capacity.

### Comparison note
Psychology matters, but it is not the whole explanation.

### Leverage point
Stabilize one structural variable first—schedule regularity, workload boundary, or guaranteed off-time—before treating this mainly as a motivation problem.
